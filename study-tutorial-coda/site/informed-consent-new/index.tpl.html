Study background and Informed consent

Purpose

Correctly specified parametric behavior in 3D CAD models facilitates the creation of design variations and allows for reusing CAD models. At present, we are running a study to observe how designers use and perceive constraints. For this, we will use Autodesk Fusion 360. We would like to learn how constraints are understood by designers, and which features are desirable and useful to have when engineering parametric 3D CAD models.

It is important to note we are not evaluating your individual performance, but the performance of the software. You are free to refuse to participate and you may discontinue participation at any time. As such, you are free to stop the study at any point in time, and to decline answering any of the questions without further explanations.

Study procedure

The study will consist out of 3 main phases, as follows:

  1. Training: during this phase, we will explain the Autodesk Fusion 360 interface (optional) and explain what constraints are and why they are useful in 3D CAD modeling. Next, we will give you a training task in the same format as the actual tasks during the study and show you how to successfully complete this task. You can repeat this procedure to get familiar with the software.
  2. Tasks: during this phase, you will perform several tasks where you will be asked to make 3D CAD models parametric according to a given parametric specification. For each task, you will be given a 3D model and a before-after illustration. Your task will consist of adding constraints to the model to ensure the model behaves as specified. For each task, there will be a time limit specified in the task description. After finishing a task, or after the allotted time has elapsed, you will move to the next task.
  3. Debriefing: after completion of the tasks, we will go to the final phase of the study, in which we will go over a debriefing survey to evaluate your subjective experiences during this study.

Information we collect and Privacy concerns

We will observe how you perform the tasks. During execution of the tasks, the screen will be recorded for later reference. Moreover, your interactions (mouse and keyboard presses) are logged to a text file to allow for analysis. Your contributions will remain pseudonymous and will be treated confidentially. Because of pseudonymization, your right to request access to, or alter, your data will be technically possible only right after you have finished your participation. Your test data (i.e., performance metrics) is never eligible for alteration.

We may publish research reports that include your comments and actions but your data will be anonymous. This means your personal information will never be disclosed in our research reports.

Your data will be stored in a private cloud-based repository as provided by the infrastructure of Hasselt University until five years after the publication of the results of our study.

The Hasselt University research team thanks you in advance for your participation in this study.
